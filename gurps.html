GURPS Introduction Tutorial

Your Complete Guide to the Generic Universal RolePlaying System

Welcome to GURPS

Imagine a toolkit so flexible you could build a medieval fantasy adventure, a space opera, a modern spy thriller, or a superhero comic book story using the same core rules. That's GURPS – the Generic Universal RolePlaying System. While D&D is like a specialized tool designed specifically for fantasy adventures, GURPS is like a Swiss Army knife capable of handling any setting, any genre, any story you can imagine.

GURPS operates on the principle that good game mechanics should be invisible and universal. Whether you're swinging a sword at a dragon, hacking a computer system, or negotiating a trade agreement with aliens, the same core resolution system handles it all. This makes GURPS incredibly powerful for creative storytelling, though it requires more up-front decision-making than simpler systems.

What Makes GURPS Different

Understanding GURPS's unique approach helps you appreciate both its strengths and when it might be the right choice for your gaming needs.

Universal Mechanics

One System, Every Setting

Unlike game systems designed for specific genres, GURPS uses the same core mechanics whether you're:

  • Playing a wizard: Casting spells uses the same skill system as any other action
  • Piloting a starship: The same difficulty and success mechanics apply
  • Performing surgery: Medical procedures use familiar skill roll mechanics
  • Fighting with swords or guns: Combat follows consistent principles

Simulation Philosophy

GURPS aims to simulate reality as closely as possible:

  • Physics matters: Armor protects by absorbing damage, not making you harder to hit
  • Consequences are realistic: Getting shot actually affects your ability to function
  • Skills work logically: Being good at one thing doesn't automatically make you good at everything
  • Characters feel human: Even heroes have limitations and weaknesses

Point-Based Character Creation

Build Exactly What You Want

Instead of random generation or class restrictions, GURPS gives you a "budget" of character points to spend:

  • Attributes: Strength, Intelligence, Health, Dexterity
  • Advantages: Special abilities, talents, and beneficial traits
  • Skills: What your character knows how to do
  • Disadvantages: Flaws that give you extra points to spend elsewhere

Complete Customization

Want to play a brilliant but physically weak scientist? A strong but slow-witted bruiser? A charismatic leader who's unlucky? GURPS lets you build exactly the character concept you envision without class-based restrictions.

Scalable Complexity

Choose Your Detail Level

GURPS can be as simple or complex as your group prefers:

  • Basic GURPS: Simple rules for quick play
  • Full GURPS: Detailed simulation for maximum realism
  • Modular rules: Add complexity only where you want it
  • House rules: Easy to modify for your specific needs

Core Mechanics: The 3d6 System

GURPS uses a beautifully simple core mechanic that creates realistic probability curves and makes success feel earned rather than random.

The Basic Roll

Roll 3d6, Roll Low

Unlike most RPGs that use single dice and try to roll high, GURPS uses three six-sided dice and aims to roll equal to or under your target number:

  • Target number: Your skill level or attribute
  • Success: Roll equal to or under the target
  • Failure: Roll over the target number
  • Example: If you have Sword skill 14, you succeed on a roll of 14 or less

Why 3d6 is Superior

Rolling three dice creates a bell curve instead of flat probability:

  • 10-11 are most common: Average results happen most often
  • 3-5 and 16-18 are rare: Extreme results are appropriately uncommon
  • Predictable success rates: You can reasonably estimate your chances
  • Realistic feeling: Most attempts at familiar tasks succeed

Success and Failure Levels

Degrees of Success

GURPS recognizes that not all successes are equal:

  • Critical Success: Roll 3-4, or 5-6 if skill is 15+
  • Success by 5+: Exceptional success with extra benefits
  • Regular Success: You accomplish what you intended
  • Success by 1: Barely made it, minimal success

Degrees of Failure

  • Failure by 1: Close call, minor setback
  • Regular Failure: Didn't accomplish the task
  • Failure by 5+: Significant failure with complications
  • Critical Failure: Roll 17-18, something goes very wrong

Modifiers: Adjusting for Circumstances

Difficulty Modifiers

The GM adds or subtracts numbers from your effective skill based on circumstances:

  • +4: Very Easy (reading in good light)
  • +2: Easy (familiar task in good conditions)
  • 0: Average (standard conditions)
  • -2: Hard (challenging but doable)
  • -4: Very Hard (pushing your limits)
  • -6: Extremely Hard (near impossible)

Situational Modifiers

  • Equipment: Good tools give bonuses, poor tools give penalties
  • Environment: Darkness, noise, weather affect performance
  • Time: Rushing gives penalties, taking extra time gives bonuses
  • Character state: Injury, fatigue, or stress impose penalties

Probability and Target Numbers

Understanding Your Chances

Knowing the probabilities helps you make informed decisions:

  • Skill 10: 50% success chance (most skills start here)
  • Skill 12: 74% success chance (trained professional)
  • Skill 14: 91% success chance (expert level)
  • Skill 16: 98% success chance (world-class master)

Effective Skill Levels

Modifiers change your effective skill for that roll:

  • Sword-14 with -2 penalty: Effective skill 12 (74% chance)
  • Computer-12 with +2 bonus: Effective skill 14 (91% chance)
  • Stealth-13 with -4 penalty: Effective skill 9 (37% chance)

Character Creation: Building Your Hero

GURPS character creation is like being handed a box of components and told to build exactly the character you envision. It requires more decisions than class-based systems, but offers unlimited flexibility.

The Four Basic Attributes

Strength (ST)

  • Measures: Physical power and health
  • Used for: Melee damage, lifting, carrying capacity
  • Default: 10 (average human)
  • Cost: 10 points per level
  • Examples: ST 8 (weak), ST 12 (athletic), ST 16 (very strong)

Dexterity (DX)

  • Measures: Agility, coordination, and manual dexterity
  • Used for: Most physical skills, ranged attacks, defense
  • Default: 10 (average human)
  • Cost: 20 points per level
  • Examples: DX 8 (clumsy), DX 12 (graceful), DX 16 (superhuman)

Intelligence (IQ)

  • Measures: Reasoning ability and mental capacity
  • Used for: Most mental skills, spells, learning
  • Default: 10 (average human)
  • Cost: 20 points per level
  • Examples: IQ 8 (slow), IQ 12 (bright), IQ 16 (genius)

Health (HT)

  • Measures: Vitality, resistance to disease, and recovery
  • Used for: Consciousness rolls, disease resistance, healing
  • Default: 10 (average human)
  • Cost: 10 points per level
  • Examples: HT 8 (sickly), HT 12 (robust), HT 16 (incredibly hardy)

Secondary Characteristics

Derived from Attributes

These are calculated from your basic attributes but can be modified:

Hit Points (HP)
  • Formula: Equal to ST by default
  • Purpose: How much damage you can take
  • Modification cost: 2 points per HP
Fatigue Points (FP)
  • Formula: Equal to HT by default
  • Purpose: Energy for sustained effort
  • Modification cost: 3 points per FP
Basic Speed
  • Formula: (HT + DX) ÷ 4
  • Purpose: Initiative and movement rate
  • Modification cost: 5 points per 0.25 increase
Basic Move
  • Formula: Basic Speed (drop fractions)
  • Purpose: How far you move in one second
  • Modification cost: 5 points per level

Advantages: What Makes You Special

Types of Advantages

Physical Advantages
  • Acute Senses [2/level]: Better hearing, vision, etc.
  • Ambidexterity [5]: No penalty for off-hand use
  • High Pain Threshold [10]: +3 to resist torture, shock
  • Rapid Healing [5]: Heal faster from injuries
Mental Advantages
  • Eidetic Memory [5]: Perfect recall
  • Lightning Calculator [2]: Instant math in your head
  • Language Talent [10]: Learn languages easily
  • Mathematical Ability [10]: Bonus to math-related skills
Social Advantages
  • Charisma [5/level]: Bonus to influence others
  • Reputation [Variable]: People know and respect you
  • Status [5/level]: Social rank and privileges
  • Wealth [Variable]: Starting money and income
Supernatural Advantages
  • Magery [15]: Ability to cast spells
  • Clairvoyance [50]: See distant locations
  • Telepathy [50]: Read minds
  • Telekinesis [5/level]: Move objects with your mind

Disadvantages: Flaws That Define You

Physical Disadvantages

  • Bad Sight [-25]: Require corrective lenses
  • Lame [-15]: Reduced movement due to injury
  • One Arm [-20]: Missing or useless limb
  • Chronic Pain [-2×level]: Regular debilitating pain

Mental Disadvantages

  • Absent-Minded [-15]: Often forget important details
  • Compulsive Behavior [-5 to -15]: Must perform certain actions
  • Phobia [-Variable]: Irrational fear of specific things
  • Short Attention Span [-10]: Difficulty concentrating

Social Disadvantages

  • Bad Reputation [-5/level]: People think poorly of you
  • Enemy [-Variable]: Someone actively opposes you
  • Poverty [-Variable]: Limited money and resources
  • Social Stigma [-Variable]: Society discriminates against you

Skills: What Your Character Can Do

GURPS uses a comprehensive skill system where you buy exactly the abilities you want. Unlike class-based systems, there are no restrictions on what skills you can learn.

Skill Difficulty Levels

Easy Skills

  • Base level: Attribute - 0
  • Point cost: 1 point for attribute level, 2 for +1, 4 for +2, etc.
  • Examples: Brawling, Climbing, Hiking, First Aid
  • Concept: Things most people can learn quickly

Average Skills

  • Base level: Attribute - 1
  • Point cost: 1 point for attribute-1, 2 for attribute, 4 for +1, etc.
  • Examples: Driving, Guns, Computer Operation, Fast-Talk
  • Concept: Professional skills requiring moderate training

Hard Skills

  • Base level: Attribute - 2
  • Point cost: 1 point for attribute-2, 2 for attribute-1, 4 for attribute, etc.
  • Examples: Surgery, Electronics, Law, Tactics
  • Concept: Complex skills requiring extensive education

Very Hard Skills

  • Base level: Attribute - 3
  • Point cost: 1 point for attribute-3, 2 for attribute-2, 4 for attribute-1, etc.
  • Examples: Magical spells, advanced sciences, exotic weapons
  • Concept: Highly specialized or supernatural abilities

Skill Categories

DX-Based Skills (Physical)

  • Combat skills: Sword, Guns, Bow, Karate
  • Athletic skills: Acrobatics, Swimming, Stealth
  • Manual skills: Lockpicking, Sleight of Hand, Artistry
  • Vehicle skills: Driving, Piloting, Boating

IQ-Based Skills (Mental)

  • Academic skills: History, Mathematics, Physics
  • Professional skills: Engineering, Medicine, Law
  • Investigation skills: Research, Detective, Forensics
  • Magical skills: Spell casting, Thaumatology

HT-Based Skills (Physical Endurance)

  • Endurance skills: Running, Swimming (endurance aspects)
  • Resistance skills: Poison resistance, Disease resistance
  • Recovery skills: Some healing-related abilities

Will-Based Skills (Mental Resistance)

  • Mental resistance: Meditation, Mental Strength
  • Social resistance: Resisting Intimidation or Fast-Talk
  • Supernatural resistance: Resisting mind control

Skill Defaults and Relationships

Default Usage

Many skills can be used untrained at default levels:

  • Guns default to DX-4: Anyone can try to shoot
  • First Aid defaults to IQ-4: Basic medical help
  • Driving defaults to DX-5: Most people can drive poorly

Skill Relationships

Related skills can substitute for each other at penalties:

  • Sword can default to Knife-2: Both are melee weapons
  • Electronics defaults to Engineer-6: Related technical fields
  • Languages default to each other: Related languages at -2 to -4

Building Skill Packages

Professional Templates

Common character types and their typical skills:

Soldier Template
  • Primary combat: Guns (Rifle), Tactics, Soldier
  • Secondary: First Aid, Camouflage, Survival
  • Physical: Hiking, Climbing, Swimming
  • Total cost: Approximately 30-40 points
Scholar Template
  • Academic core: Research, Writing, Teaching
  • Specialization: 2-3 related knowledge skills
  • Languages: 2-3 relevant languages
  • Total cost: Approximately 25-35 points
Investigator Template
  • Investigation: Criminology, Forensics, Observation
  • Social: Interrogation, Streetwise, Fast-Talk
  • Physical: Stealth, Shadowing, Photography
  • Total cost: Approximately 35-45 points

Combat: Realistic and Tactical

GURPS combat emphasizes realism and tactical decision-making. Unlike systems where fighters automatically get better at avoiding being hit, GURPS separates the ability to hit from the ability to avoid damage.

Combat Sequence

Initiative and Turns

  1. Determine initiative: Roll 3d6 + Basic Speed
  2. Act in order: Highest result goes first
  3. One second per turn: Each combat round is one second
  4. Declare and resolve: State action, then roll dice

Actions Available

Attacks
  • Attack: Strike with weapon or unarmed
  • All-Out Attack: Bonus to hit, but no defense
  • Feint: Fake attack to reduce enemy defense
  • Move and Attack: Move and strike at penalty
Defenses
  • Block: Use shield or weapon to stop attack
  • Dodge: Move out of the way
  • Parry: Deflect attack with weapon
  • All-Out Defense: Bonus to all defenses, no attacks
Other Actions
  • Move: Change position without attacking
  • Ready: Draw weapon or prepare item
  • Aim: Improve accuracy for ranged attacks
  • Wait: Delay action to interrupt enemy

Attack and Defense Mechanics

Making an Attack

  1. Roll to hit: 3d6 vs weapon skill
  2. Apply hit location: Random or targeted body part
  3. Defender chooses defense: Block, Dodge, or Parry
  4. Roll defense: 3d6 vs defense value
  5. If defense fails: Attack hits, roll damage

Damage and Protection

Weapon Damage
  • Thrust weapons: Based on ST (knife, spear)
  • Swing weapons: Higher damage from leverage (sword, axe)
  • Ranged weapons: Fixed damage (gun, bow)
  • Modifiers: Weapon adds to basic damage
Armor Protection
  • Damage Resistance (DR): Subtracts from damage
  • Location-specific: Different body parts can have different armor
  • Damage types: Some armor better against certain attacks
  • Realistic protection: Armor stops damage, doesn't make you harder to hit

Injury and Consequences

Hit Points and Injury
  • Hit Points: Total damage you can absorb
  • Injury effects: Penalties based on current HP
  • Knockdown: Major hits can knock you down
  • Stunning: Some hits cause temporary incapacitation
Shock and Pain
  • Shock penalties: Recent injuries affect performance
  • Pain tolerance: Some characters handle pain better
  • Cumulative effects: Multiple injuries compound
  • Recovery time: Realistic healing rates

Tactical Options

Advanced Combat Maneuvers

Targeted Attacks
  • Called shots: Target specific body parts at penalty
  • Vitals targeting: High damage but very difficult
  • Limb targeting: Disable arms or legs
  • Equipment attacks: Strike weapons or armor
Special Attacks
  • Grappling: Grab and control opponents
  • Disarming: Remove enemy weapons
  • Tripping: Knock enemies down
  • Combination attacks: Multiple strikes in sequence

Environmental Factors

  • Terrain: Footing affects movement and defense
  • Visibility: Darkness imposes attack penalties
  • Range: Distance affects accuracy
  • Position: Higher ground provides advantages

Magic and Spells

GURPS treats magic as a collection of skills, just like any other abilities. This creates a flexible, realistic approach to supernatural powers that can scale from subtle mysticism to world-shaking sorcery.

The Magic System

Magery Advantage

To cast spells, you need the Magery advantage:

  • Magery 0 [15 points]: Can cast spells but at -5 penalty
  • Magery 1 [15 points]: Cast spells normally
  • Magery 2 [25 points]: +1 to all spell skills
  • Magery 3 [35 points]: +2 to all spell skills, can learn exclusive spells

Spells as Skills

Each spell is a separate Very Hard IQ-based skill:

  • Prerequisites: Must know certain spells before learning others
  • Spell skill: Higher skill means easier casting and more options
  • Energy cost: Spells drain Fatigue Points (FP)
  • Time to cast: Most spells take 1-3 seconds

Spell Categories

Animal Spells

  • Beast Summoning: Call animals to aid you
  • Animal Control: Command natural animals
  • Shape Shifting: Transform into animal forms
  • Beast Speech: Communicate with animals

Body Control Spells

  • Lend Health: Transfer HP to others
  • Heal: Restore lost Hit Points
  • Cure Disease: Remove illnesses
  • Neutralize Poison: Counter toxins

Communication and Empathy

  • Mind Reading: Read surface thoughts
  • Telepathy: Mental communication
  • Emotion Control: Manipulate feelings
  • Suggestion: Plant ideas in minds

Earth Spells

  • Shape Earth: Mold stone and soil
  • Earth to Stone: Strengthen materials
  • Stone to Earth: Weaken structures
  • Earthquake: Shake the ground

Fire Spells

  • Ignite Fire: Start flames
  • Create Fire: Conjure magical flames
  • Fireball: Hurl explosive fire
  • Resist Fire: Protection from heat

Water Spells

  • Purify Water: Clean contaminated liquids
  • Create Water: Conjure pure water
  • Shape Water: Control liquid movement
  • Breathe Water: Function underwater

Casting Mechanics

Basic Spell Casting

  1. Meet prerequisites: Know required spells
  2. Spend energy: Pay FP cost
  3. Roll spell skill: 3d6 vs spell skill level
  4. Apply results: Success means spell works

Spell Energy Costs

  • Low cost spells: 1-2 FP (Light, Ignite Fire)
  • Medium cost spells: 3-5 FP (Heal, Fireball)
  • High cost spells: 6+ FP (Major healing, Teleportation)
  • Maintenance: Some spells cost FP per minute to maintain

Spell Failure

  • Normal failure: Spell doesn't work, still costs FP
  • Critical failure: Spell backfires, possible dangerous effects
  • Energy recovery: Rest 1 FP per 10 minutes normally
  • Meditation: Recover FP faster with Meditation skill

Alternative Magic Systems

Ritual Magic

  • Longer casting times: Minutes or hours instead of seconds
  • More powerful effects: Greater flexibility and scope
  • Group casting: Multiple mages can contribute
  • Material components: Require special ingredients

Clerical Magic

  • Divine source: Power comes from deities
  • Moral restrictions: Must follow divine codes
  • Different spell lists: Focus on healing and protection
  • Prayer requirement: Must maintain favor with deity

Threshold-Limited Magic

  • Mana limitations: Magic availability varies by location
  • Spell stability: Powerful magic affects magical field
  • Research requirements: New spells must be invented
  • Academic approach: Magic as scholarly pursuit

Game Master's Guide to GURPS

Running GURPS requires a different approach than class-based systems. The GM has more tools for customization but also more decisions to make about how the world works.

Setting the Framework

Choose Your Complexity Level

Lite GURPS
  • Use basic rules only: Ignore optional complexity
  • Simple character creation: Templates instead of point-buy
  • Fast combat: Skip detailed hit locations and injuries
  • Streamlined skills: Combine related skills into broad categories
Standard GURPS
  • Core rules: Full Basic Set rules
  • Detailed characters: Point-buy with advantages/disadvantages
  • Tactical combat: Full combat system with all options
  • Comprehensive skills: Detailed skill system
Advanced GURPS
  • Optional rules: Add systems from supplements
  • Detailed simulation: Maximum realism and complexity
  • Specialized systems: Custom rules for specific genres
  • Extended campaigns: Long-term character development

Define Your Campaign's Scope

Technology Level (TL)
  • TL 0: Stone Age (before 8000 BC)
  • TL 1: Bronze Age (3500-1200 BC)
  • TL 2: Iron Age (1200 BC-500 AD)
  • TL 3: Medieval (500-1450 AD)
  • TL 4: Age of Sail (1450-1730)
  • TL 5: Industrial Revolution (1730-1880)
  • TL 6: Mechanized Age (1880-1940)
  • TL 7: Nuclear Age (1940-1980)
  • TL 8: Digital Age (1980-2025)
  • TL 9: Microtech Age (2025-2070)
  • TL 10+: Robotic Age and beyond
Supernatural Elements
  • No magic: Realistic, historical campaigns
  • Low magic: Rare, subtle supernatural elements
  • Standard magic: Magic exists but isn't dominant
  • High magic: Magic is common and powerful
  • Super magic: Magic dominates daily life

Character Point Budgets

Campaign Power Levels

Ordinary Humans (25-50 points)
  • Concept: Realistic modern people
  • Examples: Students, clerks, typical citizens
  • Limitations: Few special abilities
  • Advantages: Very relatable characters
Competent Individuals (100-150 points)
  • Concept: Skilled professionals
  • Examples: Soldiers, doctors, detectives
  • Capabilities: Good at their specialties
  • Versatility: Some secondary skills
Exceptional Characters (200-300 points)
  • Concept: Action movie protagonists
  • Examples: Special forces, master thieves, brilliant scientists
  • Abilities: Multiple areas of expertise
  • Special traits: Some minor advantages
Heroic Characters (400-500 points)
  • Concept: Larger-than-life heroes
  • Examples: Fantasy heroes, pulp adventurers
  • Capabilities: Exceptional in multiple areas
  • Powers: Significant advantages and abilities
Legendary Characters (750+ points)
  • Concept: Mythic heroes, demigods
  • Examples: Superheroes, ancient legends
  • Scale: World-changing capabilities
  • Powers: Supernatural or superhuman abilities

Running Different Genres

Fantasy Campaigns

  • Magic system: Choose appropriate spell complexity
  • Technology level: Usually TL 3-4
  • Races: Define non-human character options
  • Social structure: Medieval or ancient societies

Modern Campaigns

  • Technology: TL 8 equipment and capabilities
  • Skills emphasis: Professional and technical skills
  • Realism level: How cinematic vs. realistic
  • Supernatural: Hidden or non-existent

Science Fiction

  • Technology level: TL 9+ with advanced equipment
  • Space travel: Define FTL rules and limitations
  • Aliens: Design non-human species and cultures
  • Psionics: Mental powers as alternative to magic
  • Social evolution: How society changed with technology

Horror Campaigns

  • Fragility: Characters more vulnerable than usual
  • Sanity rules: Mental stress and breaking points
  • Limited information: Mystery and uncertainty
  • Atmosphere: Emphasis on mood and tension
  • Consequences: Actions have serious, lasting effects

Session Management

Pacing and Flow

  • Skill rolls: Don't roll for everything, focus on important actions
  • Combat speed: Use simplified rules for minor encounters
  • Character moments: Give everyone chances to use their specialties
  • Realistic consequences: Actions have logical results

Customization Guidelines

  • House rules: Modify systems that don't fit your campaign
  • Optional rules: Add complexity only where needed
  • Character approval: Review builds to ensure campaign fit
  • Advancement: Award points based on character growth and story contribution

Your First GURPS Character

Creating your first GURPS character can seem overwhelming with all the options available. Here's a step-by-step guide to building a functional, interesting character.

Step-by-Step Character Creation

Step 1: Character Concept

Start with a simple, clear idea:

  • Archetype: What role do they fill? (warrior, scholar, scout, leader)
  • Background: What did they do before adventuring?
  • Personality: One or two defining traits
  • Goals: What do they want to achieve?

Step 2: Determine Point Budget

Ask your GM for the campaign's point total:

  • 100-150 points: Competent professional
  • 200-250 points: Exceptional individual
  • 300+ points: Heroic character

Step 3: Set Attributes

Spend roughly 40-60% of points on attributes:

Balanced Character (150 points)
  • ST 11 [10]: Slightly above average strength
  • DX 12 [40]: Good coordination
  • IQ 12 [40]: Above average intelligence
  • HT 11 [10]: Good health
  • Total: 100 points
Specialist Character (150 points)
  • ST 10 [0]: Average strength
  • DX 14 [80]: Excellent coordination
  • IQ 11 [20]: Above average intelligence
  • HT 10 [0]: Average health
  • Total: 100 points (focused on physical skills)

Step 4: Add Advantages (10-30 points)

Choose 2-4 advantages that fit your concept:

Combat Character
  • Combat Reflexes [15]: +1 to initiative, hard to surprise
  • High Pain Threshold [10]: Ignore shock penalties
  • Fit [5]: Extra Fatigue Points
Social Character
  • Charisma 2 [10]: +2 to influence skills
  • Voice [10]: +2 to singing and public speaking
  • Attractive [4]: +1 to social interactions
Mental Character
  • Eidetic Memory [5]: Perfect recall
  • Language Talent [10]: Learn languages easily
  • Mathematical Ability [10]: +3 to math skills

Step 5: Take Disadvantages (-10 to -40 points)

Flaws that give you extra points to spend:

Physical Disadvantages
  • Bad Sight (Nearsighted) [-25]: Need glasses
  • Hard of Hearing [-10]: -4 to hearing rolls
  • Lame (Limping) [-10]: Reduced movement
Mental Disadvantages
  • Absent-Minded [-15]: Forget details under stress
  • Curious [-5 to -15]: Must investigate mysteries
  • Overconfidence [-5 to -15]: Overestimate abilities
Social Disadvantages
  • Sense of Duty [-2 to -15]: Feel responsible for others
  • Honesty [-10 to -15]: Cannot lie or cheat
  • Code of Honor [-5 to -15]: Personal moral code

Step 6: Buy Skills (remaining points)

Focus on your character's specialty, then add breadth:

Combat Character Skills
  • Primary weapon [8-12 points]: Sword-15 or Guns-15
  • Secondary combat [4-8 points]: Shield-13 or Tactics-12
  • Athletic skills [6-10 points]: Climbing, Swimming
  • Utility skills [4-8 points]: First Aid, Survival
Social Character Skills
  • Influence skills [12-16 points]: Fast-Talk, Diplomacy
  • Information skills [8-12 points]: Streetwise, Savoir-Faire
  • Knowledge skills [6-10 points]: Current Events, Politics
  • Backup skills [4-8 points]: Basic combat or technical

Playing Your First Session

GURPS sessions flow differently than class-based RPGs. Understanding these differences helps you participate effectively and enjoy the unique aspects of the system.

What to Expect

Session Pace

  • Slower character creation: More options mean more decisions
  • Detailed combat: Tactical options take time to resolve
  • Skill-focused gameplay: Many scenes revolve around skill use
  • Realistic consequences: Actions have logical, lasting effects

Player Responsibilities

  • Know your character: Understand your abilities and limitations
  • Think tactically: Consider realistic approaches to problems
  • Ask questions: Clarify rules and situations
  • Contribute ideas: GURPS rewards creative problem-solving

Common Situations

Skill Challenges

Much of GURPS gameplay involves skill rolls:

Simple Skill Use
  • Player: "I want to hack into their computer system"
  • GM: "Roll against Computer Hacking, but it's a secure system, so -4"
  • Player: "My skill is 14, so I need 10 or less. I rolled 9 - success!"
  • GM: "You break through their security and access the files"
Extended Challenges
  • Complex tasks: May require multiple rolls
  • Time pressure: Limited attempts before consequences
  • Team efforts: Multiple characters contributing
  • Varying difficulties: Different aspects have different modifiers

Combat Encounters

Tactical Thinking
  • Position matters: Range, cover, and height affect outcomes
  • Equipment choices: Right tool for the right job
  • Defensive options: When to block, dodge, or parry
  • Target selection: Where to aim for maximum effect
Damage Consequences
  • Injury effects: Wounds affect performance
  • Realistic recovery: Healing takes time
  • Equipment damage: Gear can be destroyed
  • Knockout potential: One good hit can end a fight

Tips for New Players

Character Optimization

  • Focus on concept: Build character personality first
  • Specialize smartly: Be very good at a few things
  • Take useful disadvantages: Flaws should create roleplay opportunities
  • Don't min-max excessively: Extreme builds can be unplayable

Gameplay Strategies

  • Use your skills: Look for opportunities to apply your expertise
  • Think realistically: Consider what would actually work
  • Collaborate effectively: Combine different character strengths
  • Manage resources: Fatigue and equipment are limited

Learning the System

  • Start simple: Use basic rules until comfortable
  • Ask for help: GURPS veterans love sharing knowledge
  • Reference materials: Keep skill lists and basic charts handy
  • Practice between sessions: Review character abilities

Specialized Subsystems

Why Choose GURPS?

GURPS isn't the right system for every player or every campaign, but when it fits, it offers unmatched flexibility and depth.

GURPS Excels When...

Flexibility is Paramount

  • Genre mixing: Fantasy characters in space, time travelers, crossovers
  • Unique concepts: Characters that don't fit standard classes
  • Realistic simulation: Campaigns emphasizing logical consequences
  • Long-term development: Characters that grow and change significantly

Players Want Control

  • Character customization: Build exactly what you envision
  • Skill specialization: Be uniquely good at specific things
  • Background integration: Mechanics that reflect character history
  • Meaningful choices: Decisions that have real consequences

Realism Matters

  • Physics-based: Rules that follow natural laws
  • Skill-based progression: Improvement through practice
  • Realistic combat: Armor protects, injuries matter
  • Logical outcomes: Actions have believable results

Consider Other Systems When...

Simplicity is Key

  • New players: Groups unfamiliar with RPGs
  • Casual play: Infrequent sessions or pickup games
  • Fast action: Preference for quick resolution over detail
  • Genre conventions: Want familiar fantasy/sci-fi tropes

Specific Genre Focus

  • D&D-style fantasy: Classes and levels feel right
  • Superhero adventures: Specialized systems might fit better
  • Horror campaigns: Dedicated systems create better atmosphere
  • Narrative focus: Story games with different priorities

Making the GURPS Decision

Questions to Ask Your Group

  • Complexity tolerance: How much detail do you want?
  • Character concepts: Do you have ideas that don't fit standard classes?
  • Campaign scope: Will you stick to one genre or mix elements?
  • Realism preference: Do you want logical, physics-based rules?
  • Learning investment: Are you willing to learn a complex system?

Starting Small

  • GURPS Lite: Try simplified rules first
  • One-shots: Test the system with short adventures
  • Familiar genres: Start with settings you know well
  • Gradual complexity: Add advanced rules as you learn
  • Experienced GM: Learning is easier with knowledgeable guide

Your GURPS Journey Begins

GURPS represents a different philosophy of roleplaying – one that prioritizes flexibility, realism, and player agency over simplicity and convention. It's not necessarily better or worse than other systems, but it offers unique advantages for the right players and campaigns.

What You've Learned

  • Universal mechanics: One system for any genre or setting
  • Point-based creation: Build exactly the character you envision
  • Skill-based gameplay: Success through competence, not luck
  • Realistic simulation: Rules that follow logical principles
  • Infinite customization: Modify any aspect to fit your needs

Next Steps

  • Download GURPS Lite: Try the free simplified rules
  • Create a character: Use the point-buy system to build someone interesting
  • Find a group: Look for GURPS players in your area or online
  • Start simple: Begin with familiar genres and basic rules
  • Expand gradually: Add complexity as you learn and grow

The GURPS Promise

GURPS doesn't promise to be the easiest system to learn or the fastest to play. Instead, it promises to give you the tools to create exactly the character you want and to play in exactly the world you envision. It treats players as creative partners rather than passengers, offering unprecedented control over both character and story.

Whether you're exploring the furthest reaches of space, delving into ancient dungeons, solving modern mysteries, or facing cosmic horror, GURPS provides the framework to make those adventures feel real, meaningful, and uniquely yours.

The system's depth rewards investment. The more you learn about GURPS, the more you discover what's possible. Characters can be anyone from any background with any combination of skills and traits. Campaigns can span genres, time periods, and realities. Stories can focus on intimate character drama or world-spanning epic adventure.

Welcome to GURPS – where every character is unique, every story is possible, and the only limits are your imagination and your willingness to roll three dice.

In GURPS, you don't just play a character – you become exactly who you choose to be, in any world you dare to explore.
